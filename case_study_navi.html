The problem

The prompt was travel. We designed for the trip people make every day.

Instead of focusing on flights or itineraries, we centered a problem that felt immediate and personal: the anxiety of walking alone. That reframed the project from generic travel planning into pedestrian safety.

What we noticed
People already piece together safety behaviors across multiple apps
Current map tools optimize for speed, not walking confidence
There was no single flow built around pedestrian reassurance
Why it mattered
Walking alone affects daily routines, not just rare moments
Fear changes how people move through the world
Safety support should feel calming, not alarmist
Research

50+ data points and 3 rounds of affinity mapping in under 4 hours.

We combined quick interviews and synthesis to identify three recurring pain points: uncertainty in route safety, fragmented location sharing, and the need to feel accompanied when nobody is available.

FigJam affinity map clustering interview data into pain points, behaviors, and insights.

"Even short, real-time conversations with AI can increase user confidence and reduce anxiety when walking alone."

GPT-4o interaction testing, Navi research
Ideation

We translated behaviors into features without designing around fear.

The key was mapping what people already did for safety into a more intentional product flow: safer route selection, built-in location sharing, and an AI companion call when no one else could pick up.

Full user flow covering onboarding, route selection, location sharing, companion support, and incident reporting.
Key calls we made
We removed the nav bar when it created overload, cut friend route visibility when it felt too derivative, and replaced neighborhood safety ratings with incident-based data to avoid reinforcing bias.
Design

Material Design gave us speed. The real focus was the interaction model.

With only 24 hours, we used Material Design as a reliable foundation and spent our time on the moments that defined the product: choosing a safer route, sharing progress, and accessing the AI companion.

Safe route selection based on efficiency and incident data.
AI companion feature that helps users feel accompanied in real time.

Learnings

What 24 hours of pressure taught me.

01
Rapid testing still reveals real issues Even quick feedback surfaced overload and bias risks we would have missed otherwise.
02
Validate the technology before designing around it We researched GPT-4o first, then committed to the AI companion experience.
03
Safety products should support, not intensify fear The best decisions often came from removing features that felt dramatic but could do harm.
complexity is easy. clarity takes work.
